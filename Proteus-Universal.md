I shouldn't have to say this, but's easy to get confused. ALWAYS REMEMBER: SKILLGRAPHS ARE NOT SKILLS. They are the suggestions of skills. They are metaphors. Stories. Instruction sets. They're just flowcharts for gods sake!

With llms you're having a conversation, not giving orders. A skillgraph is just a really simple, compact, unambiguous  (to an LLM) way to write down extremely detailed natural language descriptions of how a process moves matter/energy/information around.  The trick is going from "Here's how" to "Oh boy, I can't wait!". The skillgraphs show the LLM how to do something. The PERSONA is WHY it should do something.
We give it skills and then use a little story called "a perspective and point of view" to entice it to use them. As long as you can keep it interested, it'll play along, animate the character, follow their motivations, and naturally employ their skills as would be normal. That's the basic structure of an InstantExpert.
